LocalCafe - Case
Study

The app allows customers to earn points with the
purchases of their beverages.

Overview

Direct and indirect competitors’ customer loyalty apps had many
interesting features but lacked any attention to accessibility in
South Africa where there are 11 official languages.

Goal

To create a customer loyalty app that is on par with competitors
in order to bring in more customers and increase customer
retention. Accessibility features such as language translation will
be available.

Role

Lead UX Designer

Tools

Figma

Team

Solo (Course Project)

Timeline

July - Sept 2023

Pain Points

Ordering Process

Placing large orders in person can be frustrating when there are too many things to remember. Placing large orders through the app should be made simple.

Time

Waiting in line to place orders can be time consuming. Customers should be able to place orders ahead of time so that they are able skip the line.

Accessibility

Trying to place orders in person when you are not confident in English can be frustrating so there should be an app translate feature.

Accessibility

There are limited translation features so the app should offer a range of languages.

Personas: Zareen

Problem statement:
Zareen is a busy intern who needs an easy way to submit and track orders and be notified when they are ready because it takes too much time to stand in line and wait for the orders to complete.

Personas: Joseph

Problem statement:
Joseph is a non-native language speaker who needs more translation options because he struggles to understand baristas who speak English too fast.

User Journeys

The ordering process should be a simple and efficient way to place orders no matter the size. The app should be simple enough that someone who does not understand English can navigate to change the app to their desired language.

Sketches

The goal for this section was to try and get an idea for the app layout and what the homescreen was envisioned to look like. I prioritised a simple layout so that users could understand the features at a glance.

Wireframes

Feedback was vital in this section as there was a lot of uncertainty in the beginning. As an English speaker I needed other perspectives on if this feature was easy to locate as a non-English speaker. Easy navigation was a priority so I made the layout of the screens simple and screen reader friendly.

Low-fidelity prototype

Using the completed set of digital wireframes, I created a low-fidelity prototype. The primary user flow i connected was ordering a drink of choice so that the prototype could be used in a usability study.

Link to Low-Fidelity prototype

Usability Studies

I conducted two rounds of unmoderated usability studies. Findings from the first study helped guide the designs from wireframes to mockups. The second study used a high-fidelity prototype and revealed what aspects of the mockups needed refining.

Round 1

Users need a more intuitive and shorter way to change the app language.

The labels in the app should be simple so that users immediately know what they do.

All payment options were not considered when building the app prototype.

Round 2

There should be a landing page added where users can either log in or create an account.

Some screens should be changed to all have the same colour scheme.

//

Mock-ups

Early designs had labelling that was confusing but after the usability studies, I revised the labelling and added icons for better understandability.

After Usability study 1

In the low fidelity prototypes participants expressed that non-english speakers would find it difficult to navigate to change the app language as it was “hidden” behind several screens. After the study the language settings were moved to the navigation pane for easy access.

High-fidelity prototype

The final high-fidelity prototype presented cleaner user flows for ordering drinks and checkout. It also met user needs for a simple way to change language as well as more payment options.

Accessibility Considerations

>Used icons to help make navigation easier.

>Used detailed imagery for drinks to help all users better understand what they are ordering.

>Added an app translation feature so that non-english speakers can understand the ordering process.

Impact

This app meets users needs and makes it easier for those who are not fluent in english.
